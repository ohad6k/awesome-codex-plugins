# Digital PR Survey Campaigns Examples

Use these patterns to draft campaign plans, methodology notes, and pitches.

## Campaign Brief Pattern

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Story angle | Remote managers report higher meeting overload than office managers |
| Audience | Workplace reporters, HR publications, productivity blogs |
| Data source | Survey of 1,000 US managers, segmented by work model |
| Citable asset | Report page with charts, question wording, methodology, and downloadable CSV summary |
| Pitch hook | New data shows the work model where managers lose the most hours to meetings |

## Methodology Note Pattern

```
Data source: [panel, customer data, public dataset, expert survey]
Sample: [number, geography, respondent criteria]
Field dates: [dates]
Question design: [exact or summarized question wording]
Weighting/exclusions: [if any]
Limitations: [what the data cannot claim]
```

## Reporter Pitch Skeleton

```
Subject: New data on [specific beat-relevant finding]

Hi [Name],

I saw your recent coverage of [specific topic]. We just analyzed [data source] and found [one finding that fits their beat].

The most useful details for your readers:
- [finding 1]
- [finding 2]
- [method detail that builds trust]

Full methodology and charts are here: [URL]

Happy to share a local/industry cut if useful.
```

## Common Fixes

| Weak Plan | Better Plan |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Survey people about our product category" | "Survey buyers about the decision pain reporters already cover" |
| "Pitch everyone with one statistic" | "Segment findings by beat and geography" |
| "Hide methodology below the fold" | "Give methodology its own section and anchor" |
