# Digital PR Survey Campaigns Knowledge

Core concepts for earning editorial links with surveys, studies, and data-led PR.

## Key Concepts

### Newsworthy Data

Survey results are linkable when they reveal a timely, surprising, or useful pattern that a publisher can turn into a story. A thin statistic with no audience consequence is not enough.

### Methodology As Trust

Journalists and editors need to know who was surveyed, how many responses were collected, when the data was collected, how questions were phrased, and what limits apply.

### Report Page

The link target should package findings, visuals, methodology, share-ready assets, and clear citation language. The page should remain valuable after initial outreach ends.

### Story Segmentation

The same dataset may support several angles: industry, geography, role, demographic, controversy, trend, or practical advice. Segment pitches by beat and audience.

### Exclusives And Embargoes

High-value reporters may need advance access, a unique angle, or an exclusive slice of the findings. Use this sparingly and track commitments.

## Helpful Tools

- Survey or panel tools for response collection.
- Spreadsheet, SQL, Python, or BI tools for cleaning and analysis.
- Charting or design tools for report visuals.
- Media databases, search operators, and social search for reporter research.
- Outreach CRM for pitches, embargoes, follow-ups, and placements.
- Analytics and backlink tools for measuring coverage and links.
