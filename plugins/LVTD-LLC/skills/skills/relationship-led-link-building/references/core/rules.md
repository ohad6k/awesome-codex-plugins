# Relationship Led Link Building Rules

Use these rules when planning relationship-first campaigns.

## Core Rules

### 1. Map People Before Asks

- Identify the person, organization, audience, prior contact, and likely motivation.
- Note what they publish, promote, need, and avoid.

### 2. Give Specific Value First

- Share, cite, quote, include, introduce, or invite in a way that fits their work.
- Do not use fake compliments or generic engagement as "relationship building."

### 3. Design Collaboration Around Mutual Audience Value

- Expert content should make participants look useful to their audience.
- Group interviews need a clear topic, good questions, and promotion plan.
- Partner assets need role clarity and ownership.

### 4. Ask In Proportion To Trust

- Cold: ask for input, correction, or permission, not a big favor.
- Warm: ask for contribution, quote, share, or resource suggestion.
- Partner: ask for co-promotion, link updates, or ongoing collaboration.

### 5. Track Relationship Health

- Record useful actions taken, responses, contributions, links, shares, and future openings.
- Avoid over-asking the same people.

## Red Flags

- The plan calls a bulk email list a relationship network.
- The "value first" action is merely flattering the prospect.
- Expert participants have no reason to care about the finished asset.
- The campaign asks for links before the participant sees value.
- Relationship notes are not tracked anywhere.
