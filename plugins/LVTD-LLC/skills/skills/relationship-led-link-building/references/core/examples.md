# Relationship Led Link Building Examples

Use these examples to design warm link opportunities.

## Relationship State Table

| State | Evidence | Appropriate Next Step |
|-------|----------|-----------------------|
| Unknown | No prior contact | Follow work, cite/share if genuinely useful |
| Aware | They responded or engaged | Ask for a small input or correction |
| Contributor | They gave a quote or answer | Share final asset and make promotion easy |
| Partner | Repeated collaboration | Explore co-created assets or reciprocal promotion |
| Advocate | Voluntarily shares or links | Protect relationship; avoid over-asking |

## Expert Survey Ask

```
Subject: Quick expert input on [specific question]

Hi [Name],

I'm putting together a practical resource for [audience] on [topic]. Your work on [specific article/project] shaped one of the questions.

Would you be open to answering this in 2-3 sentences?
[Question]

I'll credit you with your preferred link and send the finished piece before promotion.
```

## Value-First Actions

| Prospect | Useful Action |
|----------|---------------|
| Publisher | Cite their guide in a relevant resource hub |
| Expert | Invite a quote on a topic they own |
| Partner | Feature joint customer use case |
| Community | Sponsor, answer questions, or maintain a resource list |

## Bad Pattern

"I liked your article. Please link to mine." This is not relationship-led; it is a cold pitch with a generic compliment.
